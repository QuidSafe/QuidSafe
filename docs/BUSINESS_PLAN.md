# QuidSafe — Business Plan

## Executive Summary

QuidSafe is a mobile-first app that solves a £multi-billion problem: 5.4 million UK sole traders have no idea what they owe in tax until January when it's too late. By connecting to their bank via Open Banking and using AI to categorise transactions, QuidSafe tells them in plain English exactly how much to set aside each month.

**Market:** 5.4M UK sole traders, growing 15% YoY (gig economy)
**Price:** £9.99/mo or £89.99/yr
**Margins:** 90%+ gross margin
**Break-even:** ~1,500 paying users (~£9k MRR)
**Year 1 target:** 10,000 users, 1,500 paying, £108k ARR
**Year 2 target:** 80,000 users, 20,000 paying, £1.4M ARR

### Key Assumptions
- 10-15% free-to-paid conversion rate
- 5% monthly churn on Pro subscribers
- 70% choose annual billing (lower churn)
- £0 marketing spend in first 3 months (organic only)
- Solo founder until £30k+ MRR, then first hire

## Funding Strategy

**Bootstrap-first approach.** Total launch cost is under £2,000. The product reaches profitability at ~1,500 paying users. No external funding needed unless pursuing aggressive growth.

If raising: Pre-seed of £50-100k at Month 6-9 to fund marketing + first hire. Revenue-based financing (e.g., Clearco, Uncapped) is also viable given predictable subscription revenue.

## Risk Assessment

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|------------|
| HMRC changes MTD rules | Medium | Medium | Modular tax engine, easy to update rates |
| TrueLayer pricing increases | Low | Medium | Abstract banking layer, can switch to Yapily |
| Competitor launches similar product | High | Medium | First-mover in plain-English niche, low price |
| AI categorisation errors | Medium | High | Human review fallback, user corrections, rules engine |
| Low conversion to paid | Medium | High | 14-day trial converts well — users see value before paying, no dead-weight free users |
| Data breach | Low | Critical | Encryption at rest, RLS, regular security audits |

## Exit Scenarios (3-5 year)

1. **Acquisition by neobank** (Monzo, Starling, Tide) — £2-5M at 20k+ paying users
2. **Acquisition by accounting software** (Xero, Sage, Intuit) — £5-15M at 50k+ paying users
3. **Lifestyle business** — £150-300k/yr net profit at 20k paying users, solo founder
4. **Raise Series A** — If growth justifies it, raise £1-3M to expand into Ltd companies + EU markets
